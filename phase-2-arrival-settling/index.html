Phase 2: Arrival & Initial Settling

3-4 Weeks in Chiang Mai

Upon arrival in Chiang Mai, your immediate priorities are settling into your accommodation, establishing basic services, and connecting with the recovery community. This phase typically spans 3-4 weeks and sets the foundation for your longer-term stay.

Airport Arrival Checklist

  • □ Enter on 60-day visa exemption (automatic stamp)
  • □ Get cell phone plan at airport
  • □ Exchange money or use ATM (note: 220 THB fee per withdrawal)
  • □ Arrange transportation to accommodation (Grab app or pre-arranged pickup)

First 3 Days: Quiet Settlement

  • □ Rest and adjust to new environment
  • □ Visit nearby grocery store
  • □ Download and set up Grab app for local transportation
  • □ Explore immediate neighborhood safely

Set Up Basic Services

  • □ Arrange cleaning service (500-800 THB for 2-3 hours)
  • □ Find and establish with primary care doctor (recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic)
  • □ Find physical therapist for ongoing care
  • □ Identify and register with local pharmacy
  • □ Set up utilities and internet if needed

Connect with Recovery Community

  • □ Attend nearby AA meetings (50+ meetings/week in Chiang Mai)
  • □ Explore Buddhist recovery and Refuge Recovery meetings
  • □ Find a sponsor
  • □ Research addiction counseling options (Counselling Thailand, The Cabin alumni network)
  • □ Connect with recovery community support network

Key Facts About Chiang Mai

  • 50+ AA meetings per week - Large recovery community
  • Housing costs: $250-500/month in Suthep area
  • Western amenities: Many western-style restaurants and services
  • ATM fees: 220 THB ($6.50) per withdrawal - use wisely
  • Transportation: Grab taxis & motorbikes very inexpensive
  • Expat community: Large English-speaking population in Suthep area

Recommended Resources

  • Join Chiang Mai Expats Facebook groups for community support
  • Primary Care: Health Care Medical Clinic (Dr. Morgan)
  • Transportation: Grab app (Thai equivalent of Uber)
  • Medical facilities: Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai (24/7 English-speaking)

What Comes Next

After settling in for 3-4 weeks, you'll transition to Phase 3 to develop your medical plan (required for DTV visa application). This involves consultations with Thai healthcare providers to establish your treatment needs.
