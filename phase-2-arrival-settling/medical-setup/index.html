Setting Up Medical Services

Establishing relationships with Thai healthcare providers (Week 1-3 of Phase 2)

Finding reliable medical care in Thailand is essential for your physical therapy, pain management, and overall health. Chiang Mai has excellent English-speaking healthcare facilities at a fraction of US costs. This guide walks you through identifying providers, scheduling consultations, and establishing a medical care team.

Finding Your Primary Care Doctor

Within your first week in Chiang Mai, identify and visit a primary care physician who can coordinate your overall healthcare.

  • ‚òê Ask your accommodation host or expat community for recommendations
  • ‚òê Recommended: Dr. Morgan at Health Care Medical Clinic (English-speaking, experienced with medical tourism and expat patients)
  • ‚òê Schedule initial consultation (~$40-90 USD)
  • ‚òê Bring your US medical records, imaging (MRI, X-rays), and surgery documentation
  • ‚òê Discuss your physical therapy needs and pain management strategy
  • ‚òê Get prescriptions refilled and verify medication availability in Thailand

Locating a Physical Therapist

Physical therapy will be a core component of your medical plan and DTV application. Finding an experienced, English-speaking PT early is critical.

  • ‚òê Ask your primary care doctor for PT referrals
  • ‚òê Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai has PT department with English-speaking therapists
  • ‚òê Alternatively, search for private PT clinics in Chiang Mai (many cater to expats)
  • ‚òê Schedule initial assessment and treatment plan discussion
  • ‚òê Verify costs: typically $25-75 USD per session (vs. $100+ in US)
  • ‚òê Confirm frequency (usually 2-3x per week for effective treatment plan)
  • ‚òê Get written documentation of treatment plan for future DTV medical letter

Identifying Emergency Medical Facilities

Know where to go in case of serious medical issues. Have backup options identified before you need them.

  • ‚òê Bangkok Hospital Chiang Mai - Premier option, 24/7 English-speaking staff, modern facilities
  • ‚òê Chiang Mai Ram Hospital - Good quality, English available, slightly lower cost
  • ‚òê Get hospital phone numbers and add to your emergency contact list
  • ‚òê Research travel health insurance coverage for emergency care
  • ‚òê Know the emergency number in Thailand: 191 (ambulance)

Setting Up a Pharmacy Relationship

Many US medications are available in Thailand at significantly lower cost. Build a relationship with a reliable pharmacy.

  • ‚òê Ask your doctor for pharmacy recommendations
  • ‚òê Look for pharmacies near your accommodation and workplace
  • ‚òê Many medications available without prescription in Thailand (confirm what requires prescription in your home country)
  • ‚òê Get contact information for emergency pharmacy access 24/7
  • ‚òê Confirm cost of your regular medications in Thailand vs US
  • ‚òê Keep a list of medication names and dosages for easy refilling

Medical Setup Timeline

Days 1-3
Initial Orientation

Rest and settle into accommodation. Get local SIM card for phone access. Locate nearby convenience stores and get Grab app for transportation.

Days 4-7
Doctor Consultation

Schedule and attend first appointment with primary care doctor. Review medical history and discuss treatment needs. Get prescriptions refilled.

Days 7-10
Physical Therapy Assessment

Schedule initial PT evaluation. Discuss treatment frequency and create written treatment plan. Confirm costs and insurance coverage.

Days 10-14
Begin Treatment

Start regular PT sessions and ongoing primary care appointments. Establish routine and medication refill schedule.

Weeks 2-4
Build Medical Network

Continue regular PT and doctor visits. Consider additional specialists (pain management, orthopedics) if needed. Document all treatments.

Ongoing
Medical Documentation

Request medical letters and treatment summaries from providers for future DTV application. Maintain records of all appointments and treatments.

What to Discuss With Your Doctor

Bring a list of topics to ensure comprehensive coverage during your consultation:

  • ‚òê Your medical history and current health conditions
  • ‚òê Current medications and whether they're available in Thailand
  • ‚òê Physical therapy needs and recommended frequency
  • ‚òê Pain management strategy (medication vs. physical treatment)
  • ‚òê Medical imaging needed (MRI, X-rays, etc.)
  • ‚òê Expected duration of treatment (important for DTV: must be 6+ months)
  • ‚òê Emergency protocols and hospital preferences
  • ‚òê Fitness for activities (gym, swimming, yoga, hiking)
  • ‚òê Ability to provide medical letter for visa application later
