About This Guide

A comprehensive, step-by-step resource for planning and executing a long-term relocation to Thailand using the Digital Nomad Visa (DTV) with medical treatment justification.

Purpose of This Guide

This guide was created to document one person's detailed journey of relocating to Thailand for long-term medical treatment and recovery. It serves as a practical roadmap for anyone considering a similar path, with real timelines, actual costs, specific requirements, and institutional knowledge gathered from embassy consultations, visa specialists, and community resources.

Rather than generic visa information, this guide provides specific, actionable steps organized chronologically from pre-departure preparation through long-term settlement, complete with the documents you'll need, costs involved, and realistic timelines.

Who Is This For?

This guide is designed for:

  • US citizens considering a long-term stay in Thailand (180+ days)
  • People with medical conditions requiring extended treatment abroad
  • Those interested in the Digital Nomad Visa (DTV) as a long-term visa solution
  • Individuals planning recovery or wellness-focused relocations
  • Anyone seeking detailed, real-world information about Thai visa processes

While the guide focuses on one specific scenario (medical treatment), the framework, timelines, and process documentation can be adapted for other DTV applications.

How to Use This Guide

The guide is organized into five sequential phases that mirror the actual visa and settlement process:

Phase 1: Pre-Departure Preparation

Essential planning 7-8 weeks before departure, including gathering medical records, arranging insurance, securing documents, and research.

Phase 2: Arrival & Initial Settling

Your first 3 weeks in Thailand on the automatic 60-day visa exemption, with focus on establishing basic services and community connections.

Phase 3: Medical Plan Development

Working with Thai medical providers to create the medical documentation required for DTV visa approval (2-4 weeks).

Phase 4: DTV Application & Vietnam Trip

Compiling your complete application package and traveling to Vietnam to submit your DTV visa application (3 weeks).

Phase 5: Long-Term Settlement

After DTV approval, settling in for the long-term with your 5-year visa, establishing permanent housing and community integration.

Tip: Start with the Full Timeline for a complete chronological view, then dive into specific phases as they become relevant.
