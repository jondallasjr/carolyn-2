Vietnam Trip for DTV Application

A critical step in the DTV visa process: traveling to Vietnam to apply at the Thai embassy. This guide covers everything you need to know about your stay in Vietnam during the application processing period.

Why Vietnam?

You cannot apply for the DTV visa from inside Thailand. You must apply from outside the country at a Thai embassy or consulate. Vietnam offers several advantages:

  • Proximity: Short flight from Chiang Mai to Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City
  • High approval rates: Thai embassies in Vietnam have excellent DTV approval records
  • Processing speed: Official timeline is 5 working days; realistic average is 2-14 days
  • Affordability: Budget accommodation available at $30-40/night
  • Tourist-friendly: Easy to arrange visa and accommodation as a US citizen

Vietnam Visa Requirements

US Citizens Entering Vietnam

US citizens require a tourist visa to enter Vietnam. Unlike Thailand, there is no visa exemption.

  • Visa type: Vietnam Tourist Visa (E-visa or stamped)
  • Cost: $25 (E-visa online) or $30-50 (stamped at embassy)
  • Processing: E-visa takes 2-3 business days
  • Validity: Single-entry, typically 30 days

Recommendation: Apply for the E-visa online before traveling. It's cheaper, faster, and eliminates the need to visit an embassy before your trip.

Travel Timeline

November 10-11:

Travel from Chiang Mai to Vietnam (Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City) with your financial sponsor (Jon)

November 12-19:

Wait for DTV approval while in Vietnam (cannot return to Thailand during processing)

November 19:

Receive DTV approval; fly back to Chiang Mai

Choosing Your Destination

Hanoi

  • Thai Embassy known for fast DTV processing
  • More chaotic but vibrant atmosphere
  • Good food scene and historic sites
  • Frequent flights to/from Chiang Mai

Ho Chi Minh City

  • Thai Consulate with strong DTV track record
  • More modern, easier to navigate
  • Larger expat community
  • Better infrastructure and English speakers

Either location works well. Choose based on flight prices, personal preference, or travel companion's preference (Jon may have input).

Accommodation in Vietnam

Budget & Booking

  • Daily rate: $30-40/night for clean, safe accommodation
  • Total stay: 7-10 nights typical
  • Budget: $210-400 for accommodation alone
  • Where to stay: Central location near embassy and restaurants

Booking platforms:

  • Agoda (has Vietnam properties; Thai payment methods accepted)
  • Booking.com
  • Airbnb
  • Local hotels (often cheaper when booking direct)

Pro tip: Book a "business hotel" or "boutique hotel" rather than hostels. They're affordable, have private rooms, and quiet environments—better for resting between embassy visits.

Total Vietnam Trip Budget

Flights (Chiang Mai ↔ Vietnam): $150-250 (round-trip)
Vietnam visa (E-visa): $25
Accommodation (7-10 nights): $210-400
Food & transportation: $100-150
TOTAL BUDGET: $485-825

Note: This does not include the $340 DTV application fee (paid in Thailand before traveling)

During Your Stay: What to Expect

Day 1 (Arrival)

  • Arrive in Vietnam and clear immigration
  • Get SIM card for phone/data (optional but helpful)
  • Check into accommodation
  • Rest and adjust to new timezone

Day 2 (DTV Application)

  • Thai Embassy visit: Submit DTV application with complete document package
  • Application is now being processed (official timeline: 5 working days)
  • Embassy may request additional documents via email
  • Check email regularly and be prepared to respond quickly

Days 3-7+ (Waiting Period)

  • Cannot return to Thailand during processing
  • Explore Vietnam, rest, and relax
  • Stay accessible by email/phone in case embassy contacts you
  • Processing times vary: could be 2 days, could be 2 weeks
  • Approval arrives via email with digital approval letter

Approval Day

  • Receive approval email with DTV visa number/approval
  • Print multiple copies of approval letter (3-5 copies)
  • Book return flight to Chiang Mai
  • Prepare for border entry with printed approval

Critical Rules During Processing

⚠️ Important Restrictions

  • Cannot leave Vietnam: Do not travel to other countries while your application is processing
  • Cannot return to Thailand: Returning will invalidate your application and require resubmission
  • Must stay accessible: Keep email and phone monitored for embassy communications
  • Have all documents ready: Embassy may request additional paperwork. Be prepared to provide it within 24-48 hours

What If Processing Takes Longer Than Expected?

The official processing time is 5 working days, but realistic times vary:

  • Best case: 2-3 days approval
  • Typical case: 5-7 days
  • Worst case: 1-2 weeks (usually due to missing documents or embassy requesting clarification)

Contingency planning:

  • Budget for up to 10 days in Vietnam (not just 7)
  • Extend your hotel booking if needed (usually available)
  • Consider your visa exemption clock: Your original 60-day Thai visa exemption continues counting down, but the DTV will override it once approved

When Approval Arrives

Next Steps

  1. Check email: Official approval will come from Thai embassy with approval number
  2. Print copies: Print 3-5 copies of approval letter or digital approval
  3. Return to Thailand: Book flight back to Chiang Mai
  4. At border: Present printed approval to Thai immigration
  5. Entry: You'll receive a 180-day DTV stamp and can stay continuously for 5+ years

Practical Travel Tips

  • Pack important documents: Keep passport, document copies, and insurance in carry-on
  • Cash vs. Card: Vietnam accepts both; have some local currency (Vietnamese Dong) for small purchases
  • Travel insurance: Ensure your policy covers Vietnam and any medical needs
  • Communication: Get a local SIM card for data and messaging
  • Transportation: Use Grab app (same as Thailand) for taxis and motorbikes
  • Language: Limited English in Vietnam; download translation app or learn basic phrases
  • Health precautions: Drink bottled water, be cautious with street food if you have dietary restrictions
